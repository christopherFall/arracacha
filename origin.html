ORIGIN
1533
Arracacha is consider one of the oldest
domesticated plants in the Americas due to its great variability.

1553

Diego Molina collected at the time of the conquest an Andean plant described it as roots as thick as the arm, similar in smell and color to carrots.

1545 - 1549

Juan Perez and Diego Palomino, conquerors of Peru, sent a letter to Spain mentioning an Andean crop called racacha.

1949

The Quechua indigenous peoples of Peru used to plant 4 varieties.

1969 - 1990

The Chibchas of the Bogotá plateau used it with potatoes and corn.

1997

From Central America it spread to Mexico, Bolivia and northern Chile.

2000

Several varieties of arracacha were identified and classified, which helped to optimize cultivation under diverse agroecological conditions, particularly in departments such as Boyacá, Nariño, Cundinamarca and Antioquia. Production and marketing were increased this year.

2010

Consumption and diversification of use expanded.
Colombian gastronomy also played an important role in increasing consumption.
Ways of industrializing the crop were explored to increase its profitability, including the production of by-products such as dehydrated arracacha, which facilitates its storage.

2020

Organic and sustainable agriculture initiatives are being created.
In terms of research, AGROSAVIA and other institutions continue to develop new varieties and seek to increase their post-harvest shelf life.

Now

In recent years, the arracacha has experienced a cultural and gastronomic revaluation.
